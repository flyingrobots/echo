# Obstructions

An obstruction explains why Echo cannot lawfully produce the requested
admission, execution, reading, revelation, or retained evidence under the
present basis and posture.

## Distinctions

- **Rejection:** named law considered a candidate and lawfully declined it.
- **Obstruction:** a required support, capability, basis, aperture, budget, or
  evidence condition is not satisfied.
- **Conflict:** declared footprints or causal expectations are incompatible for
  the attempted act.
- **Internal fault:** Echo failed to uphold its own runtime invariant and may
  require quarantine or trusted recovery.
- **Absence:** requested retained material is not present; absence must not be
  silently converted into success.

These postures must remain typed and attributable. Catch-all strings erase
whether retry, authorization, repair, a new basis, or operator intervention is
appropriate.

## Evidence Posture

Retained material may be present, redacted, encrypted with an unavailable key,
missing, corrupt, outside budget, or policy-obstructed. Content identity and
semantic coordinates remain distinct: matching bytes do not prove that a
reading, receipt, or witness belongs to the requested causal coordinate.

## Capability and Support

A capability presentation is checked against its authority domain, scope,
subject, basis, expiry or revocation posture, and requested operation. Runtime
support is checked independently. Possessing a syntactically valid capability
cannot invent an unavailable observer, codec, handler, retention class, or
budget.

## Publication

Obstruction facts and receipts are evidence projections over admitted history.
They do not create a parallel mutable error database. Public surfaces should
preserve enough typed information for product adapters to map posture without
importing product nouns into Echo core.
